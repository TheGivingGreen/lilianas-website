Our Story

Some restaurants are built on a business plan. Liliana's was built on a dream.

After 30 years in the restaurant industry — starting as a young cook at the beloved Rossino's in Central West End, learning the craft of pizza and homestyle Italian from the ground up — Tim Pieri knew exactly what he wanted. Not a big concept. Not a franchise. A small restaurant with a short menu, fresh ingredients, and food made like you were a guest in his home.

"Our goal is to offer a warm and friendly atmosphere so you walk in as a guest and leave as family."

— Tim Pieri, Owner

In July 2018, Tim and his wife Kathy opened Liliana's Italian Kitchen in South County's Sappington neighborhood, in the former home of Molldeli's Delicatessen on Tesson Ferry Road. They named it after their daughter, Liliana.

What started as a dream quickly became a destination. Within months, critics were calling it one of the best new restaurants in St. Louis. Within a year, the pizza was winning awards. And for the people who live in South County, it became something better than either of those things: a regular spot. A comfortable table. A place where the staff knows your order.

Tim brings 30 years of craft to every plate — house-roasted meats sliced to order, pasta made with care, St. Louis-style pizza that's earned the title twice over, and Italian sandwiches named after characters that feel right at home on the menu. Real ingredients. No shortcuts. Food we'd be proud to serve our own family.

Because that's who you are when you walk through our door.
